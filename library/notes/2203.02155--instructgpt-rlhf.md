## Summary

The RLHF method paper (GPT-3 -> SFT -> PPO/PPO-ptx). Humility-relevant content: closed-domain hallucination halves under RLHF (41% -> 21%, printed in sec 1 p.3; sizes collapsed per Fig. 4 caption); TruthfulQA "about twice as often" truthful+informative (no printed numbers — Figure 6 bars only; acknowledgments note automatic TruthfulQA metrics initially overstated PPO gains); under an Instruction+QA prompt PPO models "err on the side of being truthful and uninformative rather than confidently saying a falsehood" (sec 4.2). Counter-nuances: SFT bar is LOWEST on hallucination (~0.10-0.12, figure-read, below PPO ~0.21); sec 4.3 documents over-hedging "partly because we instruct labelers to reward epistemic humility"; false-premise acceptance noted qualitatively. No calibration metric anywhere in the paper.

## Extracted numbers

1 row in `meta-analysis/evidence/effects.csv` (area=hallucination, comparison=pretrain_vs_rlhf, PDF-verified): closed-domain hallucination 41 -> 21. TruthfulQA deltas NOT extracted (figure-only). Counterpoint row: generic RLHF improves this humility surface.

Anchors draft sec 6.1's construct-specificity point (RLHF damages the probability readout, not every humility surface) and the sec 2 RLHF method-origin citation; the over-hedging quote is an early primary-source statement of reward-induced unwarranted hedging.
