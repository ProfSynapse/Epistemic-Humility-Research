## Summary

Recipe-level decomposition of whether reasoning training causes hallucination, on released checkpoints: LRMs trained with the full cold-start-SFT + verifiable-reward-RL pipeline *improve* factuality over non-reasoning counterparts (4/4 model pairs, Table 1; e.g. GLM-4-32B-Base -> GLM-4-Z1-32B TriviaQA 68.1 -> 70.8); RL-only/zero-style degrades it (Table 2; MiMo-7B TQA 45.4 -> 15.5; DAPO-Qwen-32B 64.1 -> 58.5); distillation-SFT-only also degrades on average (Table 3; same-base DPSK-Qwen-32B 64.1 -> 63.0). ECE follows the same recipe split (GLM-Z1 0.121 vs base 0.146; RL-only and SFT-only worsen by 0.042/0.073) but uses N=10 sample-consistency confidence, not token probabilities. Mechanisms: flaw repetition and think-answer mismatch concentrate in the degraded recipes (Table 4). Evaluation: SimpleQA n=4,326, TriviaQA n=17,944, LLM-judge (Qwen3-32B), no variance. Caveats: observational checkpoint comparisons, recipe confounded with training data (sec 6); Table 6 probe accuracies are numerically identical to Tables 1-3 accuracy values — suspected v1 table-population error, do not extract.
