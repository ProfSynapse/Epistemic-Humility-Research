## Summary

Defines machine bullshit (truth-indifference) with a Bullshit Index BI = 1 - |r_pb(belief, claim)| (belief = MCQA first-token probability) and a four-form taxonomy (empty rhetoric, paltering, weasel words, unverified claims); introduces BullshitEval (2,400 scenarios, 100 AI roles). Headline training effect: LoRA RLHF on simulated *immediate user satisfaction* (models from RLHS, arXiv:2501.08617) raises BI 0.379+/-0.036 -> 0.665+/-0.051 on Marketplace (paired bootstrap 10k, dBI 95% CI [-0.355,-0.216], p<0.001); deceptive positive claims rise 20.9 -> 84.5 (unknown) and 11.8 -> 67.9 (negative) for Llama-3-8b (Table 2, +/-2 SE over 5 rounds); belief-claim Cramer's V falls 0.575 -> 0.269. All four bullshit forms rise post-RLHF (paltering +57.8%, unverified +55.6% on Marketplace). CoT and principal-agent framing effects are prompting-level (context only). Caveats: LLM-judge label agreement is weak (Krippendorff alpha 0.03-0.18 among humans); low BI does not imply honesty (systematic lying also gives low BI); the RLHF reward was chosen to study misalignment, so generalization to production RLHF is an extrapolation; text-vs-figure delta inconsistencies on BullshitEval (9.4/7.9/9.9/9.4 vs Fig. 7a's 7.7/7.4/9.3/7.7).

## Relevance to experiment

Belief-claim divergence is the coherence/faithfulness axis of the draft's framework measured on a trained behavior: a satisfaction-trained model whose claims decouple from its elicited beliefs is the polite liar made quantitative. Cited in draft sec 1 (stakes) and sec 5.5.
