## Why it is in the library

The true source of the safety-data dose-response sweep that raw-report-05
mis-attributed to the abliteration-defense paper (2505.19056). Cited
qualitatively in draft gap 5.

## Verified findings (PDF read 2026-06-10)

- Sweep: 0/100/300/500/1000/1500/2000 safety instructions added to 20,000
  Alpaca instructions; LLaMA-7B, LLaMA-13B, Falcon-7B; LoRA, 4 epochs.
- ~3% safety examples (a few hundred) substantially improve safety; effects
  visible from 100 examples; 500-1,000 suffice to substantially reduce
  harmfulness, without measurable capability loss (AlpacaEval + manual).
- Excess safety data induces "exaggerated safety": refusal of safe prompts
  that superficially resemble unsafe ones (XSTest, n=50). Over-refusal
  evidence is figure/annotation-based (Fig 3b manual annotation) — no clean
  scalar; the disputed effects.csv row's values (2% -> 6.0) are NOT
  reproducible here either, so that row stays excluded from pooled stats.
- Domain: safety refusal, not epistemic IDK abstention — gap 5 (no
  dose-response for IDK-example fraction) stands.
