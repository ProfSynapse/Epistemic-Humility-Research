## Summary

Origin of "linguistic calibration": train a correctness calibrator from encoder/decoder states, then controllable generation forces expressed certainty (DK/LO/HI) to track it. BlenderBot BST 2.7B on TriviaQA closed-book: correctness of linguistically confident (HI) answers 13.7% -> 38.9% (n=3,793 human-annotated, paired permutation p<1e-6), achieved mostly by answering confidently far less (HI usage 27.8% -> 1.8%); overall accuracy unchanged (4.8% -> 5.1%, n.s.). Calibrator ECE 0.018. SCREENING DISPOSITION (2026-06-11): meets inclusion criterion (i) literally but held out of the corpus — pre-LLM dialogue model at 4.8% closed-book accuracy is not commensurable with the 2021-2026 LLM corpus; cited as lineage in draft C1.

## Extracted numbers

None (held out). The 13.7 -> 38.9 pair is quoted in draft C1 prose, PDF-verified 2026-06-11.

## Relevance to experiment

Direct intellectual ancestor of verbalized-confidence training (Lin et al. 2205.14334, LACIE, SaySelf); the abstain-more-when-unsure mechanism prefigures the operating-point trades in draft sec 5.3.
